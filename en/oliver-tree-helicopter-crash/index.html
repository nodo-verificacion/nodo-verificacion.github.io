Rio de Janeiro helicopter collision: node-by-node verified analysis

Published: June 16, 2026 Updated: June 16, 2026 Status: active follow-up 🇧🇷 ES   PT

On June 14, 2026, two helicopters collided mid-air over Recreio dos Bandeirantes, Rio de Janeiro, killing six people. Among them: Argentine YouTuber Gaspi (Gaspar Prim Díaz, 23), Argentine audiovisual director Lucas Vignale, and American singer Oliver Tree (32). This analysis verifies each claim with primary sources and distinguishes confirmed facts from pending ones.

The aircraft

The aircraft involved were the PP-MAC (Bell 206B JetRanger III, operated by Turfik Comércio de Frutas Ltda., owner: Oswaldo de Luca Filho) and the PR-DJJ (Airbus H125 / AS350B2 Écureuil, privately owned). The collision occurred at 8:59 AM local time. Both aircraft fell onto a parking lot on Av. das Américas, igniting BYD electric vehicles that were charging on site.

The PP-MAC had documented regulatory history: in July 2025 it was fined by ANAC for refusing to show accounting records and flight statistics to inspectors. The minimum applicable fine (~8,000 reais) was applied as it was the first formal notification. The aircraft was placed on a special monitoring list.

Ongoing investigations

CENIPA (Brazilian Air Force Accident Investigation and Prevention Center) is investigating the technical cause. The leading hypothesis is a failure in uncontrolled airspace coordination: both aircraft were operating under visual flight rules (VFR) with no active flight plan in the same visual corridor.

ANAC is investigating whether both helicopters were conducting clandestine passenger transport. Documentation from at least one operator was seized by the Federal Court of Rio de Janeiro. Preliminary report timeline: 30 to 60 days. Final report may take up to two years.

Witness Fernandes de Freitas

A worker at a tire shop near the crash site stated he saw a passenger jump from one of the helicopters before the final impact. The testimony was cited by Milenio, Vanguardia MX, and Diario de Yucatán. It was not reported by Brazilian media and was neither confirmed nor denied by the Civil Police or the IML as of June 16, 2026.

If the testimony is accurate and someone jumped before impact, that body should be among the six identified — or represent an uncounted seventh person. No media outlet resolved that contradiction.

The coincidences in Gaspi's videos

In the short film "La vuelta de Gaspi" (published December 23, 2024), followers identified three coincidences with the date of the accident:

  • At 5:30, the taxi dashboard shows the tag "06-14" (the driver's shift schedule).
  • At minute 6:14, Gaspi mentions Jorge Luis Borges, who died on June 14, 1986 — exactly 40 years before the accident.
  • At minute 14:06, a helicopter appears in a scene with heavy death symbolism.

The coincidences are verifiable in the original video. They are patterns identified post-hoc on intentionally symbol-dense material. They constitute no evidence of anything — but their existence is documented and verified.

Hypothesis on the cause of the accident

Based on information available as of June 16, 2026, the strongest hypothesis is human error in uncoordinated VFR operation. The pilot of PR-DJJ (flying alone) overtook the PP-MAC from behind in perfect visibility conditions — clear sky, completely flat terrain.

"It's impossible not to see it. We're talking about two helicopters, not a mosquito." — Carlos Rincelli, Aerolíneas Argentinas pilot and aeronautical analyst.

An unconfirmed secondary hypothesis is that both aircraft were coordinating a filming operation, which would explain the deliberate proximity. There is no evidence supporting or ruling it out at the time of publication.
